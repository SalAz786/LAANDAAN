03 — Itinerary

Seven days
in the city

Eight days. Two confirmed club nights. One jazz institution. Seven neighbourhoods. Zero wasted mornings.

01
Fri · 24 Jul
Arrival — Gatwick to Camden

Both fly in independently and meet at Gatwick arrivals. Board the Gatwick Express to Victoria (30 min), then Northern line to Camden Town. Check in from 15:00. Walk along Regent's Canal to Camden Market. Dinner at Chin Chin Labs. Then — because London doesn't wait — straight to the Jazz Café for Carl Craig All Night Long. Detroit techno legend, all night, five minutes from the flat. The perfect first night.

02
Sat · 25 Jul
East London — Markets, Streets & KOKO

Morning: Brick Lane — bagels, vintage shops, Beigel Bake. Then Spitalfields Market and a walk through Shoreditch. Stop at the newly opened Village Underground Rooftop — Shoreditch's most talked-about new space, built on top of the venue with decommissioned train carriages on the roof. Afternoon: Urban Revivo on Neal Street, Covent Garden — the fashion store everyone is saving to their London lists. Evening: dinner at Brat by Tomos Parry — open-fire Basque cooking, txakoli wine, one of the city's most exciting restaurants. Book well ahead. Night: KOKO — Acid House Palace: Paranoid London (Live). Sat 25 Jul, 22:00–05:00. Iconic Camden venue, serious lineup. 19+ bring photo ID.

03
Sun · 26 Jul
Recovery, Primrose Hill & Electric Cinema

Slow morning at the flat — coffee, croissants, morning light. Late brunch at Greenberry Café on Regent's Park Road. Walk up Primrose Hill for the best panoramic view of London. Afternoon: stroll through Regent's Park and visit the open-air theatre (check What's On). Evening: cocktails at Nightjar (book ahead) then a late film at the Electric Cinema, Notting Hill — London's most atmospheric vintage cinema. Armchair seats, table lamps, drinks delivered to you. London's coolest night in.

04
Mon · 27 Jul
Tate Modern, White Cube, Borough Market & Le Pont de la Tour

Morning: Tate Modern (free) and the Turbine Hall. Cross the Millennium Bridge. Afternoon: lunch at Borough Market, then a short walk to White Cube Bermondsey — one of the world's most respected contemporary art galleries, housed in a vast industrial space on Bermondsey Street. Free entry, always a major exhibition on. Walk the Southbank to Butler's Wharf for pre-dinner drinks at Dandelyan / Lyaness at Sea Containers, then dinner at Le Pont de la Tour — Tower Bridge lit up directly in front of you.

Tue · 28 Jul
Notting Hill, V&A & Quaglino's

Morning: Portobello Road Market — antiques, vintage, street food. Coffee at Electric Diner. Browse MG & Co (Matilda Goad) on Ebury Street — hardware, homewares and lighting in a beautiful Belgravia shopfront. Afternoon: V&A Museum (free). Chelsea walk. Dinner: Gold or Zephyr in Notting Hill (from the @unlockingldn list — great taste, no commitment required). Late: Quaglino's in Mayfair for cocktails and live music — the grand Art Deco dining room with a DJ that takes over after midnight.

Wed · 29 Jul
King's Cross, German Gymnasium & Mayha

Morning: Coal Drops Yard, King's Cross — Thomas Heatherwick's extraordinary shopping destination on the canal. Granary Square waterfront. Lunch at the German Gymnasium — one of London's most spectacular dining rooms, a soaring Victorian gymnasium converted into a Grand Café with European cooking, excellent cocktails and a terrace on the square. Afternoon: Islington, Upper Street and Camden Passage antiques. Evening: dinner at Mayha, Marylebone — London's omakase counter of the moment. Intimate, beautiful, extraordinary cooking. Book weeks ahead. Late: XOYO.

Coal Drops YardGerman GymnasiumIslingtonMayha · MaryleboneXOYO

Thu · 30 Jul
Hackney Wick & Ronnie Scott's

Morning: coffee at Climpson & Sons on Broadway Market — London's best independent roasters. Broadway Market Saturday (check — may be Sat, adjust days). Walk London Fields and along the Regent's Canal back toward Camden. Afternoon: Hackney Wick — studios, street art, the Olympic legacy neighbourhood. Evening: head to Soho for dinner, then a night at Ronnie Scott's on Frith Street — the greatest jazz club in the world. Opened 1959, two shows nightly, red-lit room, world-class musicians every single night. Book the late show (23:30) and stay until 03:00. A perfect penultimate London night.

06 — Essentials

Good to
know

🇮🇹 T1 — Italian Passport
No Visa Required
Italian (EU) passport — visa-free entry to the UK for up to 6 months. No ETA currently required for EU nationals. Check gov.uk before travel as rules may update.
🇬🇧 T2 — British Passport
British Citizen
British passport — right of abode in the UK. No visa, no restrictions, no time limit. Simply arrive. The UK is home.
Currency
Pound Sterling
Cards accepted almost everywhere — contactless standard. Avoid airport exchange. Monzo or Wise highly recommended. ATMs on every high street.
Getting Around
Oyster / Tap
Tap your contactless card on every bus and tube. Daily cap ~£8.50 zones 1–2. Black cabs are pricey but excellent. Uber for late nights.
Weather in July
18–26°C
London in late July is usually warm. Pack light layers — evenings cool quickly. A compact rain jacket is never a bad idea. Check BBC Weather daily.
Emergency
999
Police, ambulance, fire: 999. Non-emergency police: 101. NHS urgent: 111. Nearest A&E from Camden: University College Hospital, Euston Road.
